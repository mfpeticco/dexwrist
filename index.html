[1X Speed] DexWrist performs highly dynamic tasks with human-level wrist dexterity.

Abstract

We present the DexWrist, a compliant robotic wrist designed to advance robotic manipulation in highlyconstrained environments, enable dynamic tasks, and speed up data collection. DexWrist is designed to be close to the functional capabilities of the human wrist and achieves mechanical compliance and a greater workspace as compared to existing robotic wrist designs. The DexWrist can supercharge policy learning by (i) enabling faster teleoperation and therefore making data collection more scalable; (ii) completing tasks in fewer steps which reduces trajectory lengths and therefore can ease policy learning; (iii) DexWrist is designed to be torque transparent with easily simulateable kinematics for simulated data collection; and most (iv) importantly expands the workspace of manipulation for approaching cluttered scenes and tasks.
